# Polyglot Standards Audit -- 2026-06-12

Extension of the pylint cleanup to every non-Python surface: shell, the
enforcement hooks themselves, JavaScript, Jinja templates, CSS, and the
CI/Dockerfile/SQL layer. Compose files were excluded (exhaustively audited
in the 2026-06-12 dev-prod parity pass, `docs/audits/dev-prod-parity/`).

**Method.** 60-agent orchestrated audit: 7 parallel surface auditors reading
their files in full against `.claude/rules/coding.md` + `docs/coding-standards.md`,
then one adversarial verifier per high/medium finding (verifiers tried to
REFUTE each claim against the code; several verified empirically with scratch
fixtures). 124 findings total: 53 high/med (44 confirmed, 15 adjusted in
severity or detail, 0 refuted -- counts before/after severity adjustments
differ slightly) + 65 low/info. Every claim cites file:line.

**Headline.** 113 of 124 findings are catchable only by human judgment --
no mechanical linter flags them. The surfaces are already lint-clean in the
mechanical sense (disciplined quoting, zero `|safe`, zero `innerHTML` sinks,
zero dead CSS selectors, no client-side money math). The defect mass is
cross-boundary contract drift, bash errexit semantics, fail-open gates, and
fixed-in-one-place-stale-in-the-sibling divergence. Linters are therefore a
FLOOR for the future, not the fix for what was found.
